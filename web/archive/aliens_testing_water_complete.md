### 第一阶段：连接超时 (Connection Timed Out)

**时间：T+0 (坠落日)**

核心场景：

并不是轰轰烈烈的生离死别，而是信号的突然中断。

02 在火山口边缘作业，他在采集硅样本。他还在麦克风里抱怨地球的重力系数有点奇怪。下一秒，重力异常，岩层崩塌。

没有尖叫。只有飞船控制台上，代表 02 生命体征的绿色波形瞬间拉成了一条红色的直线，然后——

Connection reset by peer.

**你的编辑重点：**

- **描写 01 的废柴感：** 他甚至不知道该按哪个按钮来扫描地底。他手里拿着喝了一半的能量饮料（已经冷了），手足无措地对着控制台大喊大叫，但他喊出的所有话，都被那行冰冷的 `ssh: connect to host 192.168.1.105 port 22: Connection refused` 挡了回来。
    
- **技术细节：** 01 试图用那是本该只有两个人知道的“生日”做密码，试图用“admin”，试图用“password”。全是错的。系统在嘲笑他的无能。
    

> 草稿片段：
> 
> 他记得 02 说过：“只有公钥是最安全的。密码是给人类那种容易遗忘的生物准备的。”
> 
> 现在，02 带着唯一的私钥掉进了地心。而 01 站在坚不可摧的防火墙外，像个试图用石头砸开金库的原始人。

### 第二阶段：依赖地狱 (Dependency Hell)

**时间：T+1年 至 T+3年**

核心场景：

01 被困在飞船里。他无法启动主引擎（需要 Root 权限），也无法离开地球（没有导航数据）。

他开始面对真正的绝望：无聊。

飞船里的娱乐系统也被 02 锁了，因为 02 以前怕他工作分心。

他只能看那些他以前从来不屑一顾的书：《Thomas’ Calculus》（托马斯微积分）、《现代操作系统》、《量子加密原理》。

**你的编辑重点：**

- **从“想念”到“模仿”：** 01 开始不是为了学技术而学，是为了**听见 02 的声音**。他在阅读 02 留下的代码注释时，仿佛能看到 02 皱着眉头的样子。
    
- **技术细节：** 他开始尝试复现 02 的环境。他不再用 GUI（图形界面），强迫自己用 CLI（命令行）。他开始理解为什么 02 设置了那么复杂的 Hash Key —— 那不是为了防贼，那是 02 的**思维宫殿**。
    

> 草稿片段：
> 
> 第 400 天。
> 
> 01 终于看懂了第 127 行代码的那个递归函数。
> 
> “原来你是这么想的，”01 对着屏幕自言自语，眼泪掉在键盘上，“你把这一步写得这么绕，是为了节省那 0.03 秒的延迟？你这个强迫症。”
> 
> 他按下回车。报错减少了一个。

- **侧信道攻击（Side Channel Attack）：** 01 发现虽然没有私钥，但他可以通过分析系统在处理错误时的“微小时间差”来推导私钥的特征。

# Aliens Testing Waters / 《外星人水质测量》

## Phase 1: Connection Timed Out (连接超时)

Time: T+0

Location: Low Earth Orbit / Geological Survey Site 4 (Volcano Rim)

这杯“卡普坦星云特饮”已经彻底冷了。

那是种令人不悦的、混合了铁锈味和过饱和糖分的廉价合成液。01 盯着杯子里那层因为重力模拟器微小震动而泛起的油光，这是他今天盯着它的第四个小时。

控制台上的每一个指示灯都是绿色的。除了那一块。

那一块原本应该显示 02 的心跳、血氧和护盾完整性的区域，现在是一片死寂的红。不是闪烁的红，是那种仿佛系统已经懒得报警的、凝固的红。

屏幕中央的终端窗口里，光标在无声地闪烁。

`root@mothership:~# _`

01 的手指悬在键盘上。他的手在抖，那种抖动甚至带动了悬浮椅的扶手发出细微的咔哒声。作为一名仅仅负责“后勤与样本归档”的二级助理，他这辈子学过的最复杂的指令就是 tar -czvf（打包压缩文件）。

而 02 是专家。从sixth grade起，02 就是那个连睡觉都在背诵《Julia，美丽宝石语言》和《八年离散，从入门到放弃》的疯子。

02 曾说：“如果我没写完试卷，你一道大题也别做，等着抄。

02 曾说：“如果我出事了，你就待在船上别动，等人来救。”

但没人会来。这里是银河系的荒郊野岭，代号“地球”的蓝色水球。

“别开玩笑。”01 终于开口了，声音干涩得像是在咀嚼沙砾，“你的护盾能抗住 4000 度的岩浆。这种地质坍塌压不死你。”

他颤抖着敲下了那个他看过无数次、却从未真正理解其含义的命令。


```bash
$ ping 192.168.1.105
```

屏幕立刻跳出反馈。速度快得惊人。


`64 bytes from 192.168.1.105: icmp_seq=1 ttl=64 time=0.003 ms`
`64 bytes from 192.168.1.105: icmp_seq=1 ttl=64 time=0.003 ms`
`64 bytes from 192.168.1.105: icmp_seq=1 ttl=64 time=0.003 ms`

01 的心脏猛地收缩了一下。通了。

“我就知道，”他笑了一声，那笑声比哭还难听，鼻涕泡差点喷到屏幕上，“你这混蛋，吓死我了。你的设备还在，那就是没事。”

他迅速切到远程登录界面。这是 02 教过他的。只要连进去，就能启动备用推进器，把 02 从地底拉出来。

密码……密码是什么？

02 从来不设简单密码。02 鄙视一切小于 2048 位的加密。

“生日，”01 喃喃自语，“一定是我的入职纪念日。你上次设过这个。”

他输入：20150921

`Access Denied.`

01 愣了一下。汗水顺着他的额角滑落，滴在那杯冷掉的饮料里。

“那就试……那次我们看超新星爆发的日子。”

输入：SN2021a

`Access Denied.`

“别闹了，”01 的声音开始变调，“02，这一点都不好笑。我要启动救援程序了。告诉我密码。”

他对着空荡荡的空气大喊，对着那个依然在 ping 通的终端大喊。

没有回答。只有麦克风里传来的、来自地底深处的、极其低沉的岩层挤压声。那是地球在咀嚼。

01 咬着牙，他在键盘上敲下了那个他一直想用但被 02 骂过无数次的万能后门。

`ssh -o PreferredAuthentications=password -o PubkeyAuthentication=no alien02@192.168.1.105`

他按下了回车。

这一次，终端没有让他输密码。

它直接吐出了一行冷冰冰的、足以冻结 01 灵魂的判决书：

```
ssh: connect to host 192.168.1.105 port 22: Connection refused
```

01 呆住了。

Ping 通了。这意味着 02 的主机（那套维生装甲的电脑）还活着，还在地底深处运作。

但是 SSH 被拒绝了。

这不是网络不通。这是“**拒绝访问**”。

这意味着，那台主机已经探测到了使用者的生命体征归零，或者是……使用者亲自锁死了外部端口。

“为什么？”01 趴在控制台上，手指死死抠着边缘，“你还没死对不对？你只是……你只是不想让我看到你现在的样子？还是说……”

屏幕上突然跳出一个自动弹窗。是 02 预设的脚本。

`[SYSTEM NOTICE]: Biological integrity < 10%. Protocol 'Diamond' initiated.`

`[Protocol 'Diamond']: External access blocked. Quarantine mode active.`

`[Message to User 01]: "Don't come down. Live."`

01 看着那个单词。Live。

他突然抓起手边那杯冷掉的饮料，狠狠地砸向屏幕。褐色的液体在重力模拟器的作用下炸开，像是一朵丑陋的烟花。

液体顺着屏幕流下来，流过那行 Connection refused。

“你妈的02，”01 哭着骂道，声音在死寂的船舱里回荡，“我连微积分都不会，开飞船Live，我怎么 Live？”

他瘫坐在地上，看着那一地狼藉。

除了《同济四》《同济四下》《同济四配套习题集》被饮料溅湿了封面，周围的一切都安静得可怕。

这是第 0 天。

距离他成为神，还有 4015 天。

# Aliens Testing Water

## Phase 1: Connection Timed Out

Time: T+0

Location: Low Earth Orbit / Geological Survey Site 4 (Volcano Rim)

The Kapteyn Nebula Special Blend had gone cold.

It was a vile, viscous synthetic fluid that tasted of rust and supersaturated glucose. Alien 01 stared at the oily sheen shimmering on its surface, vibrating slightly with the ship’s gravity simulator. He had been staring at it for four hours.

Every indicator light on the console was a steady, comforting green. Except for one block.

The sector that should have been streaming Alien 02’s heartbeat, blood oxygen, and shield integrity was a dead, flat red. Not blinking. Not alarming. Just a solid, static crimson, as if the system couldn't even be bothered to panic.

In the center of the main screen, the terminal cursor blinked silently.

root@mothership:~# _

01’s fingers hovered over the keyboard. His hands were shaking, a tremor so violent it made the armrests of his suspension chair rattle. Click-clack. Click-clack.

He was a Level 2 Logistics Assistant. The most complex command he had ever mastered was tar -czvf to archive sample logs.

02 was the expert. 02 was the maniac who recited Planetary Geology and Quantum Encryption Protocols in his sleep.

"If anything happens," 02 had said, adjusting his exosuit before the drop, "you stay on the ship. You wait for retrieval."

But no one was coming. This was Earth, a blue waterball in the galactic boondocks.

"Quit joking," 01 finally spoke. His voice was dry, grinding like gravel. "Your shield is rated for 4000 degrees. A little tectonic collapse won't crush you."

Trembling, he typed the command he had seen a thousand times but never truly understood.

```bash
$ ping 192.168.1.105
```

The feedback was instant. Terrifyingly fast.

`64 bytes from 192.168.1.105: icmp_seq=1 ttl=64 time=0.003 ms`

`64 bytes from 192.168.1.105: icmp_seq=2 ttl=64 time=0.004 ms`

01’s heart contracted. Alive.

"I knew it," he let out a laugh that sounded more like a choke, snot bubbling dangerously close to the pristine screen. "You bastard. You scared me. The rig is up. You're fine."

He fumbled to switch to the remote login interface. 02 had taught him this. Just tab into the shell, init the auxiliary thrusters, and pull him out.

Password... what was the password?

02 despised weak encryption. "Passwords are for humans," he’d sneer. "They forget."

"Birthday," 01 muttered, sweat stinging his eyes. "My induction day. You used it last time."

Input: 20150921

Access Denied.

01 froze. A drop of sweat rolled off his nose and landed in the cold Kapteyn Blend. Plink.

"Okay. Okay. The day we watched the supernova."

Input: SN2021a

Access Denied.

"Stop it," 01’s voice cracked, rising in pitch. "02, this isn't funny. I need to run the extraction protocol. Give me the key."

He yelled at the empty air. He yelled at the terminal that was still happily pinging back life signs.

No answer. Only the low, grinding groan of tectonic plates shifting deep underground, transmitted through the comms channel. The sound of a planet chewing.

Gritting his teeth/mandibles, 01 typed the backdoor command he’d always wanted to use but was forbidden from touching.

```bash
$ ssh -o PreferredAuthentications=password -o PubkeyAuthentication=no alien02@192.168.1.105
```

He hit Enter.

This time, the terminal didn't ask for a password.

It spat out a single, frozen sentence that iced 01’s soul to the core:

```bash
ssh: connect to host 192.168.1.105 port 22: Connection refused
```

01 stopped breathing.

The ping was active. The host—the life-support computer in the exosuit—was running.

But the SSH was refused.

Not "Network Unreachable." Refused.

It meant the host was there, listening, and had actively slammed the door in his face.

"Why?" 01 clawed at the console edge, his nails screeching against the metal. "You're not dead, are you? You just... you don't want me to see? Or..."

A system modal popped up. A script 02 must have written for this exact worst-case scenario.

`[SYSTEM NOTICE]: Biological integrity < 10%. Protocol 'Diamond' initiated.`

`[Protocol 'Diamond']: External access blocked. Firewall rules updated: DROP ALL.`

`[Message to User 01]: "Don't come down. Live."`

01 stared at the word. Live.

He grabbed the cup of cold, rusty Kapteyn Blend and hurled it at the screen.

The brown liquid exploded in the micro-gravity simulation, blooming like an ugly, dark nebula. It dripped down the glass, streaking over the line Connection refused.

"Fuck your 'Live'," 01 sobbed, the sound echoing in the dead silence of the bridge. "I don't even know calculus. I don't know how to fly this ship. How am I supposed to live?"

He slid off the chair, collapsing onto the floor amidst the sticky puddle.

Next to him, a thick, pristine textbook lay on the deck. Thomas’ Calculus, 13th Edition.

A single drop of the sugary drink landed on its cover.

This was Day 0.

4,015 days remain until Supervisor initialization.

## Phase 2: Dependency Hell

Time: T+412 Days

Location: High Orbit / The Mothership’s Library Partition

The silence was the worst part.

Space isn’t silent. The ship groaned as it thermally cycled in and out of Earth’s shadow. The cooling pumps hummed. The hard drives clicked. But there was no voice.

02 used to talk to the ship. He’d curse at the compiler, sing off-key anthems about silicon lattice structures, or lecture 01 on the elegance of a well-optimized grep command.

Now, the only voice was the automated system reminder: [Maintenance Required: Sector 4 Air Filters].

01 sat on the floor of the server room. The gravity simulator was set to 0.8G to save power. He felt lighter, insubstantial, like a ghost haunting his own life.

In front of him lay the enemy: Thomas’ Calculus, 13th Edition.

The cover was stained with a brown, sticky ring from that thrown cup of Kapteyn Blend a year ago. 01 hadn’t cleaned it. It was the only new landmark in his stagnant world.

"Page 42," 01 muttered, his voice cracking from disuse. "Limits and Continuity."

He traced the epsilon-delta definition of a limit with a trembling finger.

∀ε > 0, ∃δ > 0 s.t. 0 < |x - c| < δ ⇒ |f(x) - L| < ε

"It means..." 01 squeezed his eyes shut, trying to summon 02’s voice from his memory. "It means no matter how close I get to c... I can always find a range where... where..."

He slammed the book shut. "It means you're never actually touching it! It means you're infinitely close but you're just... hovering!"

He threw the book across the room. It hit the server rack with a dull thud.

He hated it. He hated the math. He hated the abstraction.

But he picked it up. He always picked it up. Because it was the only thing 02 had touched that 01 could still "touch" back.

Time: T+890 Days

Location: Terminal 01

01 had stopped using the GUI.

The graphical interface was for tourists. It was for the Logistics Assistant he used to be.

Now, his screen was a black void filled with green text.

He was trying to fix a broken dependency in the ship’s long-range scanner driver. The error message was a nightmare of circular logic: lib_geo_scan requires lib_quantum_v2, which conflicts with lib_core_legacy installed by System.

Dependency Hell.

"Okay," 01 whispered. He hadn't slept in 30 hours. His eyes were burning. "You want lib_quantum? I'll give you lib_quantum."

He started typing. His typing was no longer the two-finger peck of the past. It was a rhythmic, angry clatter.

He opened the source code for the driver.

And there, on line 4096, he found it. A comment.

C

```
// TODO: This hack is ugly as sin, but it bypasses the
// standard checksum. If you are reading this, 01,
// stop looking at my code and go clean the filters.
// -- 02, T-minus 4 days before drop.
```

01 stopped.

His breath hitched. The cursor blinked at the end of the line, waiting.

It was like hearing a ghost laugh.

"I'm not cleaning the filters," 01 whispered to the screen, a tear cutting a clean track through the grime on his face. "I'm fixing your ugly hack, you arrogant jerk."

He didn't fix the driver that day. He spent twelve hours just staring at that comment.

He realized then that he wasn't learning code to fix the ship.

He was learning code because code was the only place where 02 was still alive.

Every function was a footprint. Every variable name (int patience_limit = 0;) was a joke. Every TODO was a promise they hadn't kept.

01 deleted the GUI launch script.

rm -rf /usr/bin/startx

"No more tourist mode," he said to the empty room. "If I'm going to get you out, I need to speak your language. I need to think like a root user."
